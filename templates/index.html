Overview

Floating HySpexBot (VORTEX AERO) is designed to fill the gap between satellite sensing and manual sampling. It offers a mobile, scalable, and AI-driven approach for nearshore water quality monitoring and plastic detection.

What it does: Measures chlorophyll, turbidity, and detects floating plastics.
Why: Early detection of algae blooms and plastic hotspots prevents ecosystem damage.
Where: Rivers, estuaries, lakes, and coastal nearshore zones.
Who benefits: Governments, research institutions, NGOs, aquaculture operators, and local communities.
- Hyperspectral sensing (400‚Äì1000 nm).
- Short-range acoustic backscatter for turbid conditions.
- Edge AI on Jetson Nano for real-time inference.
- Solar-assisted battery with energy-aware sensor sequencing.
- Cloud dashboard, map tiles, and alerting via MQTT/HTTP.

ML & Data

Edge-first ML pipeline: preprocess ‚Üí feature extraction ‚Üí fusion ‚Üí inference. Models run on Jetson and are optimized via TensorRT for speed and low power.

- Hyperspectral spectra (400‚Äì1000 nm), resampled to fixed band vectors.
- Acoustic features from short-range ping echoes.
- Environmental metadata: turbidity, water temperature, sun angle.
- Ground truth: lab-measured chlorophyll and FTIR polymer IDs.
- OpticalNet: 1D conv / MLP for spectral features.
- AcousticNet: small MLP for echo features.
- Fusion layer: attention-based reweighting conditioned on turbidity & sun-angle.
- Output: classification (plastic type) + regression (chlorophyll mg/m¬≥).
A synthetic data generator helps bootstrap training with simulated spectra across turbidity and illumination conditions. Models are then fine-tuned with small in-situ labeled datasets for robust generalization.

Technical Deep-Dive (expandable)

The electronics bay contains a 12.8V LiFePO4 battery connected to MPPT solar charge. Power rails: 5V (Jetson) via buck, 3.3V for sensors. MCU (STM32/ESP32) handles PWM for ESCs and ADC for acoustic. Use single-point grounding and shielded lines for acoustic analogs.
Ubuntu (JetPack) container with PyTorch/TensorRT, MQTT client, data ingestion daemon, and inference service. Models exported to TorchScript/ONNX and optimized for FP16. Use systemd / Docker for service management.
Train optical networks on resampled 150-band vectors with turbidity augmentation. Combine acoustic features (peak, energy, width, slope) in fusion network. Loss: cross-entropy for classification + MSE for chlorophyll regression. Domain adapt with small in-situ fine-tuning.
Pre-deployment: Spectralon white reference, dark current capture. In-field: collect paired water samples for lab Chl-a extraction (acetone method) and FTIR polymer ID. Record environmental meta (sun angle, wind, turbidity).
